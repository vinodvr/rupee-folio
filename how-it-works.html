How It Works

Understanding the investment philosophy and calculations behind RupeeFolio.

1 Unified Portfolio Model

Instead of managing separate allocations for each goal, RupeeFolio uses a simplified two-bucket approach that consolidates all goals:

Short-Term Bucket

Goals less than 5 years away

  • • 100% Arbitrage Fund
  • • Low risk, stable returns
  • • Equity taxation benefits
  • • Expected return: ~6% post-tax

Long-Term Bucket

Goals 5+ years away

  • • Configurable equity/debt split
  • • Default: 60% Equity / 40% Debt
  • • Adjustable from 20% to 80% equity
  • • Automatic glide path as goals approach

Why unified? This approach simplifies portfolio management by consolidating all goals into two buckets rather than managing separate allocations per goal. It's easier to implement and rebalance in practice.

2 Glide Path Strategy

Long-term goals automatically reduce equity exposure as they approach to protect accumulated gains from market volatility:

Years Remaining Equity Allocation
8+ years Full allocation (e.g., 60%)
5-8 years Half allocation (max 40%)
3-5 years Quarter allocation (max 20%)
0-3 years 0% equity (100% debt)

SIP calculations factor in this tapering by simulating month-by-month compounding with varying rates, ensuring accurate projections even as allocations change over time.

3 Return Assumptions

Default post-tax annual returns based on historical market performance:

Asset Class Range Default
Equity (Index Funds) 8% - 13% 10%
Debt (Money Market) 4% - 7% 5%
Arbitrage Fund 4% - 8% 6%
EPF 7% - 9% 8%
NPS 8% - 11% 9%
Inflation 4% - 8% 6%

All return assumptions can be customized in the Plan tab under "Expected Returns & SIP Step-up".

4 Fund Recommendations

RupeeFolio recommends low-cost index funds from ICICI Prudential or HDFC:

Equity Portion (Long-term goals)

Nifty 50 Index Fund Direct Growth 70%
Nifty Next 50 Index Fund Direct Growth 30%

This combination provides exposure to India's top 100 companies with low expense ratios.

Debt Portion (Long-term goals)

Money Market Fund Direct Growth 100%

Money market funds offer stability with better returns than savings accounts.

Short-term Goals (<5 years)

Equity Arbitrage Fund Direct Growth 100%

Arbitrage funds provide debt-like stability with equity taxation benefits (lower tax on gains held over 1 year).

Why index funds? Index funds have lower expense ratios (0.1-0.2% vs 1-2% for active funds) and most active funds fail to beat the index over long periods. For wealth-building, consistent low-cost returns matter more than trying to beat the market.

5 Retirement Corpus Estimation

RupeeFolio estimates a Financial Independence corpus — the amount you need to stop working. This calculation is used by the Quick Setup wizard, and is also available via the "Help me estimate" button on any retirement goal in the Goals and Plan tabs. Here's how it's calculated:

1. Retirement Monthly Expenses = Non-EMI Expenses × 70% + Healthcare

2. Healthcare = min(Monthly Income × 5%, ₹25,000)

3. Years in Retirement = 90 − FI Age

4. Corpus = Retirement Monthly Expenses × 12 × Years in Retirement

Rounded down to nearest ₹10 Lakh, minimum ₹1 Crore

Why 70% of current expenses?

Post-retirement, several costs drop naturally — no commute, no work clothes, no career-related spending. 70% is a widely-used rule of thumb for estimating retirement expenses.

Why add a healthcare buffer?

Healthcare costs rise significantly with age. The wizard adds 5% of your income (capped at ₹25,000/month) as a dedicated healthcare budget on top of the 70% expense ratio.

Configurable FI Age

You can choose your Financial Independence age between 40 and 55 (default 50). An earlier FI age means more years in retirement and a larger corpus, but also a more ambitious savings target.

Note: This is an initial estimate in today's rupees. The goal system applies 6% inflation to calculate the actual future value you'll need. EPF, NPS, and linked assets are then subtracted to determine your additional SIP requirement.

6 Retirement Goals & EPF/NPS

For retirement goals, EPF and NPS contributions are factored in separately with their own return assumptions:

  • EPF and NPS corpus (current balance) tracked as assets
  • Monthly EPF/NPS contributions from salary recorded in Cash Flow
  • Future value calculated separately using EPF/NPS return rates
  • Remaining gap filled by additional SIP in mutual funds

EPF/NPS Step-up

If your EPF/NPS contributions increase with salary (e.g., 5% annual hike), enable step-up in the retirement goal settings. This projects higher contributions over time, potentially reducing your additional SIP requirement.

7 Step-up SIP

Step-up SIP increases your monthly investment by a fixed percentage each year (default 5%):

Example: ₹10,000/month with 5% step-up

Year 1: ₹10,000/month
Year 2: ₹10,500/month
Year 3: ₹11,025/month
Year 4: ₹11,576/month

Why step-up? This aligns with expected salary growth and helps reach goals faster through the power of compounding. Starting with a lower SIP that grows over time is often more practical than committing to a high fixed amount.

9 Asset Linking Rules

You can link existing investments to goals to reduce required SIP. However, not all assets are eligible:

Excluded from Goal Linking

  • Real Estate — Illiquid; can't sell a portion when needed
  • Vehicles — Depreciating assets, not investments
  • Insurance Policies — Separate purpose (protection, not investment)
  • EPF/NPS — Retirement-specific, handled separately in retirement goals

Eligible for Short-term Goals (<5 years)

FDs, Savings Accounts, Debt Mutual Funds

Low-volatility assets suitable for near-term needs

Eligible for Long-term Goals (5+ years)

Equity MFs, Stocks, Gold ETFs, plus all short-term eligible assets

Higher volatility acceptable for distant goals

10 How SIP is Calculated

The monthly SIP needed for each goal is calculated using the annuity due formula (payment at beginning of period):

1. Future Value = Target Amount × (1 + inflation)^years

2. Blended Return = (equity% × equity_return) + (debt% × debt_return)

3. Monthly SIP = FV × r / [(1 + r)^n - 1] / (1 + r)

where r = monthly return, n = months remaining

For goals with linked assets, the future value of linked investments is subtracted from the target before calculating SIP. For step-up SIP, a simulation determines the starting amount that reaches the target when contributions increase annually.

? Frequently Asked Questions

Why 60/40 allocation for long-term goals?

This is a balanced approach that captures equity growth (historically ~10-12% returns) while debt provides stability during market downturns. The 60/40 split has been a widely-used benchmark globally. You can adjust this in Settings based on your risk tolerance — more aggressive investors might prefer 70/30 or 80/20.

Why can't I link my house to a goal?

Real estate is illiquid — you can't easily sell a portion to fund your child's education or an emergency. This tool focuses on liquid investments that can be redeemed when needed. Your house contributes to net worth but isn't available to fund goals.

Why does my SIP show "Achieved" or ₹0?

Your linked investments (current value + projected growth) already exceed the inflation-adjusted target. No additional SIP is needed — just let your investments compound until the goal date. If markets drop significantly, you may need to reassess.

Why are EPF/NPS not in my investment plan?

EPF and NPS are retirement-specific instruments with their own rules (withdrawal restrictions, tax treatment). They're automatically included in retirement goal calculations if you've added them to Cash Flow. For non-retirement goals, they're excluded since you can't easily access these funds.

What if I want a different equity allocation?

Go to the Plan tab and expand "Initial Asset Allocation (Long-Term Goals)". Adjust the equity slider from 20% to 80%. All projections will recalculate automatically. Remember: higher equity = higher potential returns but more volatility.

Is my data safe?

Yes. All data is stored locally in your browser's localStorage. Nothing is ever sent to any server — your financial information never leaves your device. However, clearing browser data will erase all saved information, so consider exporting your data periodically.
